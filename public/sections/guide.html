How to use it

CURE YOUR IDENTITY CRISIS

Stop mixing roles. This is how multitalented people actually stay organized — separate your skill trees, level each identity independently.

ROLES

Create One Character Per Role

You're not one person—you're multiple people wearing different hats. Create a character for The Developer, The Athlete, The Student, The Creator. Reduce decision paralysis by focusing on one role at a time.

  • One character per major life role (max 3-5 recommended)
  • Customize appearance to match each role's vibe
  • Each character has their own stat page and history
  • Switch focus: when you're in "Developer mode," only level that character
HABITS

Assign Habits to Characters

Habits like "workout" belong to The Athlete. "Read 30 min" belongs to The Scholar. By assigning habits to characters, you separate your skill trees and avoid mixing identities.

  • Use Check type for binary habits (workout, meditate, read)
  • Use Count for reps (pushups, pages, hours coded)
  • Use Measure for tracked amounts (water ml, km run, lines written)
  • One daily focus: pick the character with today's priority habits
QUESTS

Break Goals Into Character Quests

Each character levels up from completing quests assigned to them. If you're working on a business goal, assign it to The Entrepreneur. If it's a creative project, assign it to The Artist.

  • One quest per goal — subtasks for each action step
  • Difficulty 7–10 gives the biggest XP reward
  • Assign each quest to the character role it belongs to
  • Use voice memos to capture ideas as they come
FOCUS

Deep Work in the Sanctuary

Launch a focus session for undistracted work. Your active character sits in the study room with you and earns XP. Ideal for ADHD brain—no pressure when the timer ends.

  • Pick Countdown for timed sessions (Pomodoro-style, 25-90 min)
  • Pick Endurance to log open-ended deep work
  • Set a goal: "Code the login flow", "Write chapter 3", "Sketch designs"
  • Use ambient sounds to minimize distraction and stay in flow
